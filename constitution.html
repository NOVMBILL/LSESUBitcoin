Part 2 – LSESU Bitcoin Society: Foundational Structure

This section defines our mission, governance, and operations. Approved by the founding committee and subject to amendment through due process.

1. Group Name

This group shall be known as LSESU Bitcoin Society.

2. Group Aims and Objectives

  • To explore and educate on Bitcoin as a monetary, technological, and philosophical innovation distinct from broader cryptocurrency or blockchain projects.
  • To promote discourse on economic sovereignty, decentralization, privacy, and the implications of Bitcoin for global governance, human rights, and financial inclusion.
  • To build a high-signal, intellectually rigorous student community through salons, workshops, and real-world experiments in sovereign systems.

3. Student Group Committee Roles

PRESIDENT:
  - Convene and chair society meetings
  - Liaise with LSESU officers and staff
  - Ensure compliance with LSESU and school policies
  - Oversee strategy, vision, and symbolic integrity

TREASURER:
  - Manage finances
  - Authorize expenditures via Expense 365
  - Seek aligned sponsors and donors
  - Ensure compliance with LSESU financial procedures

SECRETARY:
  - Liaise with members and coordinate events
  - Maintain digital comms and room bookings
  - Take minutes during all official meetings

Note: All officers must be LSE students and pay membership dues.

4. Student Group Subcommittees

None established at inception. Subcommittees may be created by committee consensus as the society scales.

5. Special Activities and Requirements

  • Workshops involving open-source privacy tools, Bitcoin node setup, or Lightning demos.
  • Speaker sessions on topics related to monetary history, anti-censorship technology, and decentralized finance (Bitcoin-only).
  • No endorsement of altcoins, ICOs, NFTs, or unaligned crypto assets.

6. Additions to Part 1 (Optional Enhancements)

  • a. Introduction:
    • The Society is a non-profit, open-access, educational group focused on lawful and inquiry-based learning.
  • b. Constitutional Amendments:
    • Any amendment must be proposed by at least two committee members and passed by 2/3 of attending full members at AGM.
  • c. Membership:
    • Open to all LSE students regardless of background or ideology. No technical prerequisite required.
  • d. Annual Membership Fee:
    • Initial fee set at £3.00. May be reviewed annually.
  • e. Elections:
    • Candidates must submit a short statement of purpose at least 48 hours before AGM.
    • All elections governed by LSESU rules and chaired by a neutral officer.
  • f. Other:
    • The Society does not accept funding from organizations promoting unaligned crypto projects.
    • All sponsors must align with Bitcoin-first principles and be transparently disclosed.
